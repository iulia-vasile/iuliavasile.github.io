FQA about UX books

What are the timeless UX books that are still revelant?

Definitely "The Design of Everyday Things" by Don Norman is considered to be the bible when it comes to UX design.

Books for design team of one?

"The freelance manifesto" by Joey Korenman, is teaching you how to run a design business independently.

Are there any practical guides books?

"100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People" by Susan Weinschenk, it explains human behavior and gives real life examples. 

One practical user research book?

"Quantifying the User Experience" by Jeff Sauro and James R. Lewis. These explain how to use quantitative UXR methods in order to obtain the most effective results and insights.

How to start with information architecture?

"Information Architecture: Blueprints for the Web" by Christina Wodtke: offers various techniques for designing and organizing information on the web.
