Life events website 

Designed a life events website for a top 3 competitor in banking industry.

My role

User Research, conducting user interviews, UX design & UI design.

Team

I worked for 6 months with 4 other amazing people, passionate about design.

Branding

Figma & Photoshop (for the UI Design) and Miro & Notion (for the UX Design)

The problem

A top 3 competitor in banking industry needed to identify key moments in life and specific financial needs occurring from these. Also, they wanted to help their clients, by giving the right advice and products, in the most important moments in their life. 

Discovery phase üå™Ô∏è

We wanted to understand the problem. So that, we started to define what we want to do, and the journey started from the double diamond method. We came up with:

- our vision about the project

- a lot of research about the company that gave us the challenge

- the goals that we want to achieve

- multiple brainstorming sessions

Empathise ‚ú®

We started the immersion process. So this is what we did:

- competition review, asking who is our competition and what are their solutions and weaknesses

- user journey and stakeholder mapping. Analyze the key moments in someone's life and to make a hierarchy of importance and recurrence.

- developed interview guides. See how clients would improve in the relationship with the company and what are their actions during an important life event.

- 14 online interviews, used for qualitative research. we discussed with clients, non-clients and employees.

Convergence process of the research.

With all the collected data, we synthesized it, in order to come up with solutions. How we did that?

- developed ~ 30 key insights from the interviews

- answered the question "How might we?" and applying this method so that we can generate ideas for the project. Generated ~ 70 initial HMWs

- centralized the HMHs in the priority order

Ideate üöÄ

Write as much ideas as you can. Trust the process.

- we had an offline ideation session with 16 people and came up with 215 ideas.

- we developed a research wall with important data that we had, so that all the participants can understand the problem

- used the "6 thinking hats" ideation method (had Disney, Apple and Ritz Hotel hat) and let the crazy ideas flow

- voted the best ones

- summarize all the ideas in one place (Miro app)

Prototype üñ•Ô∏è

After the ideation session:

- each one of us created his own vision about the concept, being inspired by the voted ideas. after that, we shared our opinions and developed the final concept, from the best ones.

- created the user flow, continued with paper sketching in order to develop the wireframes

- started doing mood boards for inspiration

- made in Figma high-fidelity mockups and the prototype that we wanted to test

- we wanted to create a fresh and friendly design, a place that the user can call "a safe space"

- the goal was to address the aspect of something new, and not to make reference to the company

Test ‚úÖ

Does it work as we want? Does the user understand the purpose of the prototype? Here is where we found out...

After the prototype was build, we wanted to see its functionality through user's eyes. So that, we:

- did 3 iterations for the web version, one before, and two after the feedback

- had 8 testing interviews and gained precious feedback from all of them

- create a summary from all the feedback and discovered the patterns and pain points

- reiterate the prototype and test it again
